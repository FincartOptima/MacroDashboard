India Macroeconomic Indicators

Welcome to the India Economic Pulse Dashboard. This interactive application provides a consolidated view of three critical macroeconomic indicators — GST Collections, Bank Credit Growth, and the USD/INR exchange rate — from January 2025 to May 2026. Data has been updated from the research synthesis using authenticated sources: Ministry of Finance PIB press releases (GST), RBI fortnightly credit bulletins, and FBIL/aggregated interbank rates (INR). The headline story is more complex than it appears: record GST collections are partly an import-inflation artifact, surging credit growth is partly distress borrowing, and the rupee depreciated ~12% in 12 months.

Authentic Data Sources & Methodology

The data presented in this dashboard represents historical figures up to May 2026. Below are the official, authentic sources where these specific datasets are published by the Government of India and relevant authorities:

  • 📄
    GST Collections: Ministry of Finance, Government of India (Press Information Bureau). Monthly press releases on gross GST revenue collection.
    Verify at PIB Portal
  • 🏦
    Credit Growth (Sectoral Deployment of Bank Credit): Reserve Bank of India (RBI). Data is released monthly detailing non-food bank credit YoY growth.
    Verify at RBI Press Releases or RBI DBIE Portal
  • 💱
    USD/INR Exchange Rate: Financial Benchmarks India Pvt. Ltd. (FBIL) and RBI Reference Rates. The figures represent the monthly average reference rate.
    Verify at FBIL
